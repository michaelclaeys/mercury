What are prediction markets?

Prediction markets are financial exchanges where traders buy and sell contracts tied to real-world events. Each contract resolves to either $1.00 (the event happened) or $0.00 (it did not). The price of a contract at any given moment represents the market's collective probability estimate for that outcome.

If a contract for "Will the Fed cut rates in March?" trades at $0.72, the market believes there is a 72% probability of a rate cut. If you disagree — if your research says the probability is actually 85% — you buy that contract at $0.72 and profit $0.28 per share when it resolves to $1.00. That gap between market price and true probability is your edge.

Two platforms dominate the prediction market landscape in 2026:

  • Polymarket — The largest decentralized prediction market. Built on Polygon, settled in USDC, operating a fully on-chain central limit order book (CLOB). Polymarket offers contracts on politics, crypto, sports, culture, and global events. No KYC required for non-US users. Billions in cumulative volume.
  • Kalshi — The first CFTC-regulated prediction market exchange in the United States. USD-settled, with event contracts covering economics, weather, finance, geopolitics, and more. Kalshi offers hourly weather contracts, making it uniquely suited to high-frequency strategies. Full API access for automated trading.

Both platforms support API-driven trading, which makes them ideal targets for automated prediction market trading bots. The combination of binary pricing, event-driven resolution, and programmatic access creates an environment where algorithmic strategies can consistently outperform manual traders.

What types of prediction market trading strategies can you automate?

Prediction markets support a broader range of automated strategies than most traders realize. Binary pricing, event-driven resolution, and cross-platform fragmentation create unique opportunities absent from equity or crypto markets.

Momentum trading

Apply technical indicators like RSI, MACD, and Bollinger Bands to prediction market price feeds. When a contract's price begins trending — often driven by accumulating evidence, polling data, or shifting sentiment — momentum strategies ride the trend until indicators signal exhaustion. Mercury's trigger nodes support 12+ technical indicators applied directly to contract probability data.

Mean reversion

Identify stable-probability markets where the true likelihood has not changed, but short-term noise has pushed the price away from fair value. Buy contracts that dip below their 24-hour moving average in markets with low fundamental volatility. Weather contracts and recurring economic indicators are prime targets. Set a probability band condition (e.g., buy below $0.65, sell above $0.75) and let the bot execute the range.

News-driven trading

Monitor external data sources — RSS feeds, Twitter/X firehose, government data releases, earnings calendars — and execute trades before manual traders process the information. Mercury's news alert trigger nodes ingest real-time feeds and match keywords or sentiment scores against your market watchlist. When the CPI number drops and it differs from consensus, your bot is already buying or selling the relevant Kalshi economics contracts.

Cross-platform arbitrage

Polymarket and Kalshi frequently price the same event differently. A presidential election contract might trade at $0.54 on Polymarket and $0.58 on Kalshi. Buy the cheap side, sell the expensive side, and lock in risk-free profit on resolution. Mercury's cross-platform data bridge identifies these gaps in real time. Even 2-3 cent spreads compound when executed thousands of times. Over $40 million in arbitrage profits were extracted from Polymarket alone between April 2024 and April 2025. For a complete walkthrough with real numbers, fee calculations, and bot-building instructions, see our prediction market arbitrage bot guide.

Resolution sniping and bonding arbitrage

As contracts approach resolution, their prices converge toward $0.00 or $1.00. A contract trading at $0.96 with 2 hours until resolution offers a 4% return in 2 hours — annualized, that is extraordinary yield. Bots monitor contracts in the final hours before resolution, buying high-probability outcomes at slight discounts. The risk is a last-minute reversal; the reward is consistent, compounding micro-profits. This strategy is especially effective on Kalshi's hourly weather contracts. For advanced traders, bonding curve markets offer additional arbitrage opportunities where algorithmic pricing creates exploitable mispricings against order book markets.

Weather market scalping

Kalshi offers hourly temperature, precipitation, and wind contracts for major US cities. These markets resolve frequently, provide tight feedback loops, and respond to publicly available weather model data (GFS, ECMWF, HRRR). A bot that ingests weather API data and compares forecast temperatures against Kalshi contract strikes can scalp 5-15% returns per contract on mispriced hourly weather outcomes. Mercury supports custom API data triggers, making weather model integration straightforward.

How does Mercury's visual bot builder work?

Mercury Architect is a no-code prediction market bot builder with two ways to create strategies. The visual editor lets you drag nodes onto a canvas and connect them with wires — each node performs a specific function like monitoring a market, evaluating a condition, executing a trade, or managing risk. Alternatively, Mercury's agentic AI lets you describe a strategy in natural language and generates the entire node graph automatically. Tell it "Buy YES on any Polymarket contract where probability drops below 10 cents and 24-hour volume exceeds $50K, with a $100 max position and 20% stop-loss" — and the AI assembles triggers, conditions, execution rules, and risk nodes, wired together and ready to test.

Whether you build manually or let the AI generate your strategy, the underlying system uses four node categories, each containing multiple specialized types:

Category 01
Trigger Nodes
  • Market trigger — fires when a specific market opens, closes, or updates
  • Price threshold — fires when a contract crosses a target price
  • Probability cross — fires when probability crosses a moving average
  • Volume spike — fires on unusual volume relative to baseline
  • Time-based — fires on schedule (cron, interval, or specific datetime)
  • News alert — fires on keyword/sentiment match in real-time feeds
  • API data — fires when an external API returns a matching value
  • Technical indicators — fires on RSI, MACD, Bollinger, or custom signals
Category 02
Condition Nodes
  • Probability band — passes signal only if price is within a defined range
  • Volume filter — gates execution on minimum/maximum volume thresholds
  • Time window — restricts trading to specific hours, days, or market phases
  • Comparison — evaluates two values (greater than, less than, equals, delta)
  • Compound logic — combines multiple conditions with AND, OR, NOT operators
Category 03
Execution Nodes
  • Buy / Sell — market orders with optional slippage tolerance
  • Limit order — place resting orders at a specific price level
  • Position sizing — calculate order size from Kelly criterion, fixed %, or flat amount
  • Multi-leg — execute correlated orders across multiple markets simultaneously
Category 04
Risk Management Nodes
  • Stop-loss — exit position if contract price moves against you past threshold
  • Take-profit — lock in gains by selling when target price is reached
  • Max exposure — cap total capital deployed across all active positions
  • Kill switch — halt all bot activity instantly on drawdown or error trigger
  • Trailing stop — dynamic stop-loss that follows price in your favor

You can also describe a strategy in plain English — "Buy Kalshi weather contracts when the HRRR forecast diverges from the contract strike by more than 3 degrees, with a $50 max position and 10% trailing stop" — and Mercury's AI assistant generates the full node graph for you. Edit any node, rewire the connections, and deploy.
