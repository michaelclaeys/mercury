Article 01

The 30-Second Morning Routine

How to Read the Market Weather in 30 Seconds

Most traders open their charts and immediately look for patterns. But at HedgeIQ, we check the "weather" first. You wouldn't sail a boat without checking if a storm is coming—don't trade without checking the Gamma Regime.

The Market Regime

Look at the top left of your dashboard. This is the single most important signal we provide.

Example: Dashboard showing Negative Gamma regime with key metrics

Negative Gamma (The Storm): A "Negative Gamma" regime means dealers are "short volatility." They are forced to sell into market drops and buy into market rallies.

What this means for you

Expect volatility. Breakouts will likely run further than you think. Do not fade the trend.

Positive Gamma (The Calm): When this is green, dealers are "long volatility." They buy the dips and sell the rips.

What this means for you

Expect mean reversion. The market will feel "sticky." Buy support, sell resistance.

The Net Indicators

Below the regime, you see the "Net" boxes (GEX, Vanna, Charm). Think of these as the current wind direction.

  • Net GEX: Confirms the regime (Negative = Volatile, Positive = Stable).
  • Net Vanna: Tells you how Implied Volatility (IV) affects price.
    • Positive: Rising IV pushes price UP.
    • Negative: Rising IV drags price DOWN.
  • Net Charm: Tells you how Time affects price. Negative Charm often leads to selling pressure at the end of the trading day.

Finding Invisible Support & Resistance

GEX Analysis: Trading the Invisible Walls

Why does price often stop at a random number like $88,000 and reverse? It's usually because of dealer positioning.

The "Flip Zone" (Zero Gamma Strike)

The "Zero Gamma Strike" is the pivot point of the entire market.

Example: Flip Zone identified at $88,000 with surrounding gamma exposure

In the example above, the Flip Zone is $88,000.

  • Above $88k: The market is stable. Volatility compresses.
  • Below $88k: The "danger zone." Volatility expands.
Strategy

Use this as your bias filter. If price is $87,500, you should be biased toward shorting rallies. If price is $88,500, look to buy dips.

The Walls (Dealer Exposure)

Scroll down to the "Gamma Exposure by Strike" chart.

Green Bars (Call Walls): These act as resistance and support. Dealers are heavily invested here. If price approaches a massive green bar (like the $85k bar in the image), dealers will likely buy to defend it.

Red Bars (Put Walls): These are acceleration zones. If price falls into a large red bar zone (like $89k-$91k), dealers are short and must sell to hedge, often flushing the price lower quickly.

Article 03

Strategy Deep Dive: The Vanna Flush

How to Catch Major Reversals

Advanced Strategy

This is an advanced strategy that exploits the relationship between Volatility (IV) and Price.

What is Vanna?

Vanna measures how dealer hedging changes when Implied Volatility (IV) changes.

The Setup

  • Identify Positive Vanna: Check your dashboard. Is "Net Vanna" Positive?
  • Wait for an Event: This setup works best around events that spike IV (Fed meetings, Earnings, CPI data).
  • The Trigger: As the event passes, IV Crushes (drops rapidly).

The Mechanism

Example: Vanna Exposure chart showing positive vanna concentration

Look at the purple bars in the "Vanna Exposure" chart.

When Vanna is Positive, dealers are essentially holding long delta hedges that are supported by high volatility.

The Flush: When that volatility evaporates (IV Crush), those hedges are no longer needed. Dealers are mathematically forced to SELL their long hedges immediately.

The Result: A rapid drop in spot price, even if the news was "neutral."

Pro Tip

If you see the "Net Vanna" badge is green, and you know a big news event is ending today, watch out for a "Vanna Flush" sell-off immediately after the news drops.

Confirmation: Are We Squeezing or Crashing?

Spot-Vol Correlation: Detecting Fakeouts vs. Breakouts

Sometimes price goes up, but the internals look weak. The Spot-Vol chart tells you the truth.

Example: Spot-Vol quadrant analysis showing current market regime

Reading the Quadrants

Top Right (Green): Price Up + Volatility Up.

Signal

This is a "Gamma Squeeze" or FOMO rally. Dealers are chasing price. Strong Bullish Signal.

Top Left (Red): Price Down + Volatility Up.

Signal

This is pure panic. Support levels usually fail here. Strong Bearish Signal.

Bottom Right (Yellow): Price Up + Volatility Down.

Signal

This is a "Grind Up." It's stable, slow, and sustainable.

Bottom Left (Purple): Price Down + Volatility Down.

Signal

This is a slow bleed or profit-taking. It is not a crash, just a drift.

How to Use It

Look at the "NOW" bubble. In the image above, we are in the Green Quadrant. This confirms the current rally has strong momentum and is being fueled by option buying.
